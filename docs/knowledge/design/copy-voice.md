# Copy Voice

The site should sound like Kuba's public technical lab: personal, useful, direct, and comfortable showing work in progress.

## Voice rules

- Write like a real technical person, not a brand deck.
- Prefer concrete nouns over abstract positioning.
- Use first person when it clarifies authorship or process.
- Let uncertainty and iteration show when they are part of the artifact.
- Keep CTAs functional and plain.

## Preferred terms

- `lab`, `notes`, `artifacts`, `build logs`, `experiments`, `projects`, `writing`
- `posts` or `notes` for article lists
- `case file` or `artifact` for project pages/cards when the context is exploratory

## Avoid

- `innovative`, `cutting-edge`, `passionate`, `solutions`, `leverage`, `seamless`, `delightful`
- conversion-first CTAs like `Book a call` as the primary frame
- overexplaining credentials on the homepage
- making experiments sound more finished than they are

## Homepage tone

The homepage should quickly say what this place is: a public lab/index of software experiments, AI workflows, architecture notes, and build logs.
